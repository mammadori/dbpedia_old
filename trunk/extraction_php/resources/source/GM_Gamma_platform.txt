'''Gamma''' is [[General Motors Corporation|General Motors]]' global [[subcompact car|subcompact]] [[front-wheel drive]] [[automobile platform]].
==GM4300==
The first version of the platform was issued in 2001 with the introduction of Corsa C and was a development of [[GM4200 platform]]. The architecture was developed by [[Opel]] in Germany and was based on the [[Opel Corsa]].

Vehicles based on this platform:
* 2001-2006 [[Opel Corsa#Opel Corsa C|Opel/Vauxhall Corsa]]
* 2001-2006 Opel/Vauxhall Corsavan
* 2001- [[Opel Combo#Combo B|Opel/Vauxhall Combo]]
* 2001- [[Chevrolet Corsa]]
* 2003- [[Opel Meriva|Opel/Vauxhall/Chevrolet Meriva]]
* 2004- [[Opel Tigra#Tigra Twin Top (2004-2007)|Opel/Vauxhall Tigra TwinTop]]

==SCCS==
A new version of the Gamma platform, dubbed '''SCCS''' (Small Common Components and Systems), was codeveloped since 2002 by a team of [[Fiat]] and [[Opel]] engineers located in Turin. The platform premiered with 2005 [[Fiat Grande Punto]] and is used in 2007 [[Opel Corsa|Corsa]].

Even after the dissolution of the GM/Fiat partnership, both companies retain the rights to continue developing SCCS-derived models.

Vehicles based on SCCS platform:
* 2005- [[Fiat Grande Punto]]
* 2007- [[Opel Corsa#Opel Corsa D|Opel/Vauxhall Corsa and Corsavan]]
* 2006- [[Fiat Linea]]
* 2007- [[Fiat Fiorino|Fiat Fiorino and Qubo]]/Citroën Nemo/Peugeot Bipper
* 2008- [[Alfa Romeo MiTo]]
* 2009- [[Opel Meriva#Future|Opel/Vauxhall Meriva]]
* 2010- [[Fiat Doblò]] second generation
* 2010- Opel/Vauxhall/Chevrolet small crossover

==Gamma II==
[[GM Daewoo]] has taken responsibility for future development of the small car architecture,<ref> {{cite web|url=http://autoweek.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20051129/FREE/511290701|title=GM Daewoo wins job to develop plaform for Aveo, other small cars for GM worldwide|work=[[AutoWeek]]|accessdate=November 29 2005 |dateformat=mdy}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://autoweek.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20061113/FREE/61113003/1024/LATESTNEWS|title=Small GM Cars go Global|work=[[AutoWeek]]|accessdate=November 13 2006 |dateformat=mdy}}</ref> based on Opel Corsa D{{Citation needed|date=December 2008}}. This architecture will replace all small vehicles from GM. Therefore, GM intends this architecture to be a true global small car platform and to be versatile platform. The first model, Chevy Aveo replacement, will arrive by 2010, and other derivatives, such as small SUV, Opel Corsa & Combo, etc, could follow the Aveo.

The vehicles will be assembled at factories in [[China]], [[Germany]], [[India]], [[Korea]], [[Mexico]], [[Thailand]], [[Venezuela]], and the [[United States]].
