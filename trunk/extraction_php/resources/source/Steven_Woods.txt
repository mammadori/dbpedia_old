'''Steven Gregory Woods''' (born [[June 16]], [[1965]], [[Melfort, Saskatchewan]], is a [[Canadian]] entrepreneur. He is best known for co-founding ''[[Quack.com]]'', the first popular [[Voice portal]] platform, in 1998.

== Career ==
Motivated by Woods' desire to pull together a highly talented group of friends in one place to do something interesting for a year, [[Quack.com]] was born in 1998, funded September 1, 1999, and acquired August 31, 2000 by [[America Online]].<ref>{{cite web|url=
http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-9357905_ITM|title=Voice: The Killer App|accessdate=2008-03-13|year=2008}}</ref>

Woods is currently Site Director for [[Google]] in its [[Waterloo, Ontario|Waterloo]], [[Canada]] facility.
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.linkedin.com/in/stevenwoods
|title=Steven Woods's public LinkedIn profile|accessdate=2008-09-24|year=2008}}</ref> Previously, he served as Chief Technology Officer of [[NeoEdge Networks]], in [[Palo Alto]], [[California]].<ref>{{cite web

 | date =2008
 | url =http://neoedge.com/company/mt.htm#steven

Sorenson Distinguished Graduate Lecture|accessdate=2008-03-13|year=2008}}</ref> along with former [[America Online]], [[Netscape]], and [[Quack.com]] colleagues including [[Jeromy Carriere]]. Prior to NeoEdge, Woods was Vice President of Voice Services for [[America Online]] and [[Netscape]] - joining America Online through the acquisition of [[Quack.com]] in September, 2000<ref>{{cite web|url=http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_hb5559/is_200009/ai_n22589166
|title=Jefferson Partners' Investment in Quack.com Pays Off, as Voice-to-Web Innovator is Acquired by AOL|accessdate=2008-03-13|year=2008}}</ref>. Quack.com was notable as the first [[Voice Portal]], and held a number of key patents around interactive voice applications, including the definitive [[Voice Portal]] patent - "System and method for voice access to internet-based information"<ref>{{cite web|url=
http://www.google.com/patents?id=Z1kOAAAAEBAJ&dq=Steven+Woods|title=System and method for voice access to internet-based information
 |accessdate=2008-03-13|year=2008}}</ref>. In 1998 Woods co-founded Pittsburgh-based voice-portal infrastructure company [[Quackware]] with [[Jeromy Carriere]] and [[Alex Quilici]]. Quackware became [[Quack.com]] in 1999 and moved headquarters to [[Silicon Valley]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalmooselounge.com/press/press_5.htm
|title=Risking it all for dot-com gold in California|accessdate=2008-03-13|year=2008}}</ref>.

Before the founding of [[Quack.com]], Woods was Technical Staff Member at [[Carnegie Mellon]]'s [[Software Engineering Institute]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sei.cmu.edu/library/abstracts/news-at-sei/feature11q02.cfm
|title=SEI Architecture Practices Propel Successful Startup|accessdate=2008-03-13|year=2008}}</ref> working on technical efforts there in product line development and practical software architectural reconstruction and analysis. He holds a Ph.D and M.Math from the [[David R. Cheriton School of Computer Science]] at the [[University of Waterloo]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uw.cheerweb.net/index.php?secid=17&pageid=55&PHPSESSID=d899682e021826bda04e16e8607013d2
|title=University of Waterloo - notable companies started by alumni|accessdate=2008-03-13|year=2008}}</ref> in [[Canada]] and a B.Sc. from the [[University of Saskatchewan]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Saskatchewan#Notable_companies_started_by_alumni_and_spin-offs
|title=Steven Woods - University of Saskatchewan Alumni|accessdate=2008-03-13|year=2008}}</ref>.

Woods' Ph.D was published in 1997 as a book co-written with [[Alex Quilici]] and Qiang Yang entitled "Constraint-Based Design Recovery for Software Reengineering: Theory and Experiments"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.amazon.com/Constraint-Based-Design-Recovery-Software-Reengineering/dp/B000TB8BKG/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1205539108&sr=8-2
|title=Constraint-Based Design Recovery for Software Reengineering: Theory and Experiments|accessdate=2008-03-13|year=2008}}</ref>
