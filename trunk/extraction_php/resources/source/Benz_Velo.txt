[[Karl Benz]] followed his successful three-wheeled [[Benz Patent Motorwagen|Patent Motorwagen]]—which was patented as the first commercially available automobile on January 29, 1886 and put into production from 1886 through 1893—with the four-wheeled '''Benz Velo''' model of 1894.  The Benz Velo and the [[Duryea Motor Wagon]], patented in 1895, are credited as the first [[standardization|standardized]] cars&mdash;134 identical Benz Velos were built in 1895.

The Velo also inspired numerous copies, including [[Marshall]] (later [[Belsize]]) in [[Manchester]],<ref>Georgano, G.N. ''Cars: Early and Vintage, 1886-1930''. (London: Grange-Universal, 1990), p.24 caption.</ref> [[Star Motor Company|Star]] ([[Wolverhampton]]),<ref name="Georgano, p.24">Georgano, p.24 caption.</ref> and [[Arnold (automobile)|Arnold]] ([[Paddock Wood]], of which only twelve were built).<ref name="Georgano, p.24"/>
