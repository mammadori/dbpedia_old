The '''BMW Z9 (or Z9 Gran Turismo, Z9 GT)''' is a [[concept car|concept]] coupe introduced in 1999 by [[BMW]] at the [[Frankfurt Auto Show]]. During the 2000 [[Paris Auto Show]] a convertible variant of the Z9 was debuted. It was designed by [[Adrian van Hooydonk]], who is recently promoted to Director of BMW Group Design.

The Z9 featured a carbon fiber skin over an aluminum space frame.<ref>[http://www.press.bmwgroup.com/pressclub/us02.nsf/fwPressemeldungFrame?readform&docid=54F8A378E6605F85C1256BB9002A1355 BMW Press Release: Z9 Gran Turismo Concept Car]</ref> Much of the styling found on the current [[BMW_E63/E64|E63 6 series]] is derived from the Z9. The vehicle also featured unique gull wing doors that also opened like a conventional hinged door.

The Z9 did not make it into production, but many of its innovations did. The interior included an early concept of [[iDrive| BMW's iDrive system]], called the Intuitive Interaction Concept<ref>[http://www.press.bmwgroup.com/pressclub/us02.nsf/fwPressemeldungFrame?readform&docid=7D7A341D17E423BAC1256BB9002A13C7 BMW Press Release: BMW Group at the 2001 North American International Auto Show]</ref>. The Z9 featured the world's first [[V8]] turbo diesel for non-truck use, later produced for the BMW 7 Series 740d model.
