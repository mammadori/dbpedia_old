The '''Volkswagen Passat CC''' (also known as the '''Volkswagen CC''' in the [[United States]])<ref name="VW CC">http://www.autoblog.com/2008/04/03/volkswagen-drops-passat-sticks-with-cc-for-new-sedan/ Retrieved 11-30-2008</ref> is a 4-door [[coupé]] version of the [[Volkswagen]] [[Volkswagen Passat|Passat]]. It debuted in January 2008 at the [[North American International Auto Show]] in [[Detroit]]. The suffix '''CC''' stands for ''Comfort Coupé'' and the car is marketed by VW as a more luxurious vehicle than the standard Passat saloon.<ref>[http://www.volkswagen.de/vwcms_publish/vwcms/master_public/virtualmaster/de3/modelle/passat_cc.html?csref=home-teaser-links-passat_cc . Retrieved 04-14-2008.]</ref>

The Passat CC falls between the Passat and the [[Volkswagen Phaeton|Phaeton]] in VW's range — while based on the Passat, and sharing its wheelbase, the CC is longer (+ {{convert|27|mm|in|2|abbr=on}}), lower (− {{convert|50|mm|in|2|abbr=on}}) and wider (+ {{convert|36|mm|in|2|abbr=on}}) than the Passat.

==Market==
Volkswagen has ambitions of selling 300,000 units over a period of seven years. The company expects that 60% of these sales (about 26,000 units per year) will come from the US market.<ref>[http://www.thetorquereport.com/2008/04/vw_expects_the_us_to_be_the_bi.html Retrieved 11-30-2008]</ref>  Pricing will start at $27,100 for the sport trim level and range to greater than $40,000 for the VR6 4Motion trim level.  <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vw.com/vwfeatures/cc/en/us/ |title=Build a CC |publisher=Vw.com |date=2008-10-07 |accessdate=2009-09-28}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.motortrend.com/roadtests/sedans/112_0807_2009_volkswagen_passat_cc/specifications.html |title=2009 Volkswagen Passat CC - Specifications - First Drive |publisher=Motor Trend |date= |accessdate=2009-09-28}}</ref>

==Technical Data==
The car rests on a {{convert|2711|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} [[wheelbase]] and is, for the time being, available with a 1.8-litre [[Twincharger|TFSI]] inline-four ({{convert|118|kW|PS hp|0|abbr=on}}) or a 2.0-litre [[Straight-4|inline-four]] [[Turbocharged Direct Injection|TDI]] engine, both with [[front-wheel drive]] and 6-speed manual, or with a 3.6-litre [[VR6 engine]] ({{convert|220|kW|PS hp|0|abbr=on}}) with [[4motion]] [[four-wheel drive]] and a 6-speed twin-clutch [[Direct-Shift Gearbox]] (DSG).

The North American market will receive the 2.0T and 3.6L VR6 engine options. Manual transmission is available in the 2.0T engine option only.<ref>[http://www.vw.com/cc/completespecs/en/us/#/show_all Retrieved 11-30-2008]</ref>
