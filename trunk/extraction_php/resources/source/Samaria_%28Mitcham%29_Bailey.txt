'''Samaria (Mitcham) Bailey''' (born June 29, 1947) in [[Macon, Georgia|Macon]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]] was an instrumental figure in the civil rights movement.  She was one of the first American females of African descent to be accepted into [[Mercer University]] and the first American female of African descent to integrate  [[Central High School (Macon, Georgia)|A. L. Miller Senior High School]], an all white female school located in Macon, in 1964. Samaria graduated from Miller H.S. with honors.   Her story was featured in the best selling novel, ''[http://books.google.com/books?id=su2g0MFHfpgC&dq=stem+of+jesse+will+campbell&printsec=frontcover&source=bl&ots=5twRHj0gzj&sig=eth9eaFCljs07nktttgUQlWTpLc&hl=en&ei=bhDmSaPlJMKLtge1qvyXAg&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=1#PRA1-PA110,M1 Stem of Jesse: The Costs of Community at a 1960s Southern School]'' by [[Will D. Campbell]] and stage play, [http://books.google.com/books?id=yFY9hLu1QNAC&pg=PR22&dq=samaria+mitcham&hl=En "Combustible/Burn"], written by [http://asilverproduction.com/ Andrew Silver].
