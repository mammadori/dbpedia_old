'''Kevin Greenaugh, PhD''' (b. April 20, 1955) is an [[United States|American]]  [[nuclear engineer]] and senior manager at the National Nuclear Security Administration (NNSA) in Washington, DC, [[USA]].

Greenaugh manages close to a [http://www.opinioneditorials.com/guestcontributors/phuessy_20090211.html billion-dollar program] in nuclear deterrent research and development. He is responsible for the development of computer models and for conducting research and testing at [http://nnsa.energy.gov/news/1175.htm world-class experimental facilities]. He has developed national policy for NNSA and is one of the [https://www.hss.energy.gov/deprep/2009/TB09M05B.HTM highest-ranking officials] involved in the nuclear deterrent.

==Early life and education==
Greenaugh was born and raised in Augusta, Georgia. and graduated from [http://chronicle.augusta.com/stories/040606/ric_76219.shtml Glenn Hills High School in Augusta, Georgia in 1973]. He received his Bachelor of Science degree in Chemistry from Mercer University; Master of Science degree in Nuclear Engineering from the University of New Mexico (Albuquerque); Master of Arts degree in Public Policy from the University of New Mexico (Santa Fe); Post-Masters studies at the University of Arizona; and his Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degree in Nuclear Engineering at the University of Maryland (UMD). Dr. Greenaugh was the first African-American to receive a PhD. in Nuclear Engineering at UMD.

==Awards==
Kevin Greenaugh, PhD. was named Black Engineer of the Year for achievement in government by the A. James Clark School of Engineering, University of Maryland in 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.engr.umd.edu/news/news_story.php?id=258 |title=Alumni Honored at Black Engineer Gala News and Current Events, A.
