The '''Volkswagen Constellation''' is the [[flagship#automotive|flagship]] truck from [[Volkswagen Trucks and Buses]] line, covering the 13-57 tonne [[gross combination mass]] (GCM)<ref name=vwtbpress370/> segment.  It is [[list of Volkswagen Group factories|produced]] at [[Resende]] in [[Brazil]],<ref name=vwtbpress370/> and is primarily for the [[South America]]n market.

The truck, a "[[cab over|cab-over-engine]]" released September 2005, was designed in [[Volkswagen]]'s [[Wolfsburg]] Design Studio at [[Volkswagen Group]] Headquarters, but engineered by Volkswagen Truck and Bus, in [[Brazil]], [[South Africa]], and mainland [[Europe]] - on a rigorous 7 million kilometre test phase over a four-year period.<ref name=VW.za>{{cite web|title=Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles - Constellation|url=http://www.vwcommercial.co.za/models/constellation/|work=Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles|publisher=VWcommercial.co.za|accessdate=22 November 2009}}</ref>

In 2006, [[Renato Martins]] won the Brazilian [[Fórmula Truck]] Championship in the Constellation's first season racing.

==Variants==
The [[cabin (truck)|cab]] is available in [[daycab]]<ref name=vwtbpress370/> and [[sleeper cab]]<ref name=vwtbpress370/> configurations.

It is available in the following [[internal combustion engine]] / [[cargo|payload]] configurations: 13.180<ref name=vwtbpress370/> and 15.180<ref name=vwtbpress370/> with {{convert|180|hp|kW PS|0|abbr=on|lk=on}} [[MWM International Motores]]; 17.250,<ref name=vwtbpress370/> 24.250 6x2<ref name=vwtbpress370/> and 19.320 Titan<ref name=vwtbpress370/> with {{convert|250|hp|kW PS|0|abbr=on}}<ref name=vwtbpress370/> or {{convert|320|hp|kW PS|0|abbr=on}}<ref name=vwtbpress370/> [[Cummins]] [[diesel engine]]s.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  A further three models were subsequently launched in 2007; a 19.370,<ref name=vwtbpress370/> 25.370,<ref name=vwtbpress370/> and 31.370,<ref name=vwtbpress370/> with a [[Volkswagen Trucks and Buses]] engineered<ref name=vwtbpress370/> and MWM produced<ref name=vwtbpress370/> {{convert|370|hp|kW PS|0|abbr=on}} Volkswagen NGD 370 diesel engine.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>

==Powertrain==
===VW NGD 370 engine===
The [[Volkswagen Group]] NGD 370 [[turbodiesel]] engine premiers new attributes for the heavy trucks sector.  The high "flat" [[torque]] at low engine speeds is improved by a variable geometry [[turbocharger|turbo-compressor]]: the Multi Turbo System - MTS.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  Its [[cylinder head]] features [[multi-valve|four valves]] per cylinder,<ref name=vwtbpress370/> [[engine displacement|displaces]] {{convert|9354|cc|1|lk=on}}<ref name=vwtbpress370/> (9.3&nbsp;[[litre]]s), and generates {{convert|367|hp|kW PS|0|lk=on}} at 2,000&nbsp;rpm.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  It also features the Dual Power Brake – DPB.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>

It also features an exclusive high pressure [[fuel injection#direct injection|direct fuel injection]] system, the Hydraulic Electronic Unit Injector - HEUI<ref name=vwtbpress370/> – with electro-hydraulic digital [[Unit Injector|unit injectors]]<ref name=vwtbpress370/> and extended durability, developed by the partner company [[Siemens]]/[[VDO (company)|VDO]].<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  An important advantage is the removable wet [[cylinder liner]]s which will assist with easier maintenance and lower costs.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>

Its Multi Turbo System (MTS) turbine with variable vane geometry, is electronically controlled.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  This helps achieve faster responses, better performance, and better thermal efficiency.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  This also works as part of the engine brake retarder in the exhaust system.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  The water-cooled [[exhaust gas recirculation]] (EGR) system, coupled with the [[cylinder block]], reutilises part of the exhaust gasses, producing combustion at lower temperatures with less discharge rates.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>

It includes a new engine brake system, the Dual Power Brake (DPB) fed into the cylinder head, is electronically integrated into the turbocharger.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  It is claimed that the advantages are: increasing the operational average speed, the brake force, safety, retaking with fuel reduction, and speed on downward slopes.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  This means less gear shifts, lower maintenance costs, and helps improve the service life of the brakes and [[tire|tyres]].<ref name=vwtbpress370/>

===Transmission===
[[Clutch]]es on all variants are the hydraulically operated with pneumatic assistance single-plate diaphragm type.<ref name=VW.za_specs>{{cite web|title=Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles - Constellation specifications|url=http://www.vwcommercial.co.za/models/constellation/specifications/|work=Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles|publisher=VWcommercial.co.za|accessdate=22 November 2009}}</ref>  All are supplied by [[ZF Sachs]],<ref name=VW.za_specs/> and range in diameter from {{convert|362|mm|1|lk=on}}<ref name=VW.za_specs/> to {{convert|430|mm|1}}.<ref name=vwtbpress370/><ref name=VW.za_specs/>  The engine output is routed through a variety of [[transmission (mechanics)|transmissions]].  One option is the [[list of ZF transmissions|ZF 16S 1685 TD]] 16-speed manual synchromesh transmission.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  This latest [[ZF]] box is {{convert|80|kg|0|lk=on}} lighter than existing models,<ref name=vwtbpress370/> due to its all-[[aluminium alloy]] casings.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  It has a [[torque]] handling capacity of {{convert|1800|Nm|0|lk=on}}.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>

The 6x2 variants use a [[Meritor]] MS 23-185 tractive rear axle,<ref name=vwtbpress370/> and this has a maximum capacity of 60&nbsp;tons.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  It includes a series differential locking system when used on the VW 25.370 tractor unit.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>

The 6x2 variants also have the facility for partial or total lifting of the third axle,<ref name=vwtbpress370/> which helps achieve better traction, particularly on slopes.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>  This Extra Traction Device (ETD) ensures part of the third axle load, during partial lifting, is transferred to the tractive axle.<ref name=vwtbpress370/>

6x4 variants utilise Meritor MD/MR 25-168 tractive rear axles,<ref name=vwtbpress370/> and are located via [[Randon S.A.]] rear [[suspension (vehicle)|suspension]] ([[bogie]] type rigid shafts in tandem).<ref name=vwtbpress370/>
