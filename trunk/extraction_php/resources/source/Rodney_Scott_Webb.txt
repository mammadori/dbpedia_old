'''Rodney Scott Webb''' (June 21, 1935 - August 9, 2009) was a [[United States|American]] federal judge.

Webb was born in [[Cavalier, North Dakota]]. He earned a [[Bachelor of Science|B.S.]] from the [[University of North Dakota]] in 1957 and a [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] from the [[University of North Dakota School of Law]] in 1959. He had a private practice in [[Grafton, North Dakota]] from 1959 to 1981.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grandforksherald.com/event/article/id/129712/ |title=Rodney S. Webb |publisher=GrandForksHerald.com |accessdate=2009-08-14}}</ref>

Webb served as:
*[[Walsh County, North Dakota|Walsh County]] State's Attorney, 1967 to 1974
*Special Assistant Attorney General for North Dakota, 1970 to 1981
*[[Grafton, North Dakota|Grafton]] municipal judge, 1975 to 1981
*[[United States Attorney]] for the District of North Dakota, 1981 to 1987

Webb was a member of the United States Attorney Generalâ€™s Advisory Committee of United States Attorneys and member of the Indian Affairs Subcommittee. He retired from the North Dakota Army National Guard J.A.G. Corps with the rank of Colonel. 

Webb was nominated on May 5, 1987, by President [[Ronald Reagan]] to the [[United States District Court for the District of North Dakota]], succeeding [[Paul Benson (judge)|Paul Benson]]. He was confirmed by the [[United States Senate]] on December 19, 1987, and received his commission on December 21, 1987. He served as chief judge from 1993 to 2001. He assumed [[senior status]] on January 1, 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.court.state.nd.us/court/news/webbrodney.htm |title=Rodney Webb 1935-2009 |publisher=North Dakota Supreme Court News |accessdate=2009-08-15}}</ref>

==Family==
His son, Wade, is a state district judge in [[Fargo, North Dakota]]. Wade Webb was sworn into office by his father in 2003.

==Death==
Judge Webb died in Fargo on Sunday, August 9, 2009, aged 74, from complications of [[cancer]].<ref>[http://www.thedickinsonpress.com/event/apArticle/id/D9A04NG80/ ND Federal Judge Rodney Webb dies]</ref>
