'''Fagbug''' is a [[Volkswagen New Beetle]] owned by Erin Davies of [[Troy, New York|Troy]], [[New York]] who, in response to [[graffiti]] on her car, embarked on a trans-[[United States|American]] [[road trip]] to raise awareness of [[gay rights]].<ref name=vanity>{{cite web | url=http://www.vanityfair.com/online/style/2009/01/fagbug.html#comments | title=The Heartwarming Story of Fagbug | last=Berk | first=Brett | date=January 8, 2009 | publisher=''[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]'' | accessdate=July 1, 2009 }}</ref><ref name=oscar>{{cite web | url=http://blog.newsweek.com/blogs/current/archive/2007/12/19/driven-by-desire-and-her-fag-bug.aspx | title=Driven to Spread Awareness | last=Raymundo | first=Oscar | date=December 19, 2007 | publisher=''[[Newsweek]]''| accessdate=December 13, 2008 }}</ref><ref name=npr>{{cite web | url=http://www.npr.org/templates/transcript/transcript.php?storyId=18137377 | title=The Adventures of a Car Named 'Fagbug' | last=Stewart | first=Alison | authorlink=Alison Stewart | date=January 16, 2008 | work=[[Bryant Park Project]] | publisher=[[National Public Radio]] | accessdate=August 10, 2009 }}</ref>

==History==
On April 18, 2007, Davies, an [[art education]] graduate student at [[Sage College of Albany]], found the words "[[Faggot (slang)|fAg]]" and "U R gay" [[spray-paint]]ed on her car.<ref name=backfire>{{cite web | url=http://www.timesunion.com/AspStories/story.asp?newsdate=5/1/2007&navigation=nextprior&category=FRONTPG&BCCode=HOME&storyID=585491&TextPage=2 | title=An act of hatred backfires | last=Parry | first=Marc | date=May 1, 2007 | work=[[Times Union (Albany)|Times Union]] | publisher=timesunion.com | accessdate=August 10, 2009 }}</ref> In response, she put her studies on hold and embarked upon a [[Documentary film|video-documented]], 55,000 mile (89,000&nbsp;km) journey though 40 [[U.S. state]]s, during which she interviewed 400 people and spoke out against [[hate crime]]s.<ref name=advocate>{{cite web | url=http://www.advocate.com/issue_story_ektid45294.asp | title=Meet the bloggers: Erin Davies | last=Kroll | first=Morgan | date=June 19, 2007 | work=[[The Advocate]] | accessdate=August 10, 2009 }}</ref><ref name=curve>{{cite web | url=http://74.125.47.132/search?q=cache:S2HoAA0ltAQJ:www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-32716150_ITM+%22Activist+debuts+the+fagbug%22&cd=3&hl=en&ct=clnk&gl=us&client=firefox-a | title=Activist Debuts the FagBug | last=Smith | first=K.M. | date=September 1, 2007 | work=[[Curve (magazine)|Curve]] | publisher=[[AccessMyLibrary|accessmylibrary.com]] | accessdate=August 10, 2009 }}</ref><ref name=news9>{{cite web | url=http://capitalnews9.com/content/top_stories/114621/-fagbug--turns-hate-into-inspiration/?RegionCookie=12 | title="Fagbug" turns hate into inspiration | last=Jubie | first=Ken | date=April 22, 2008 | publisher=[[Capital News 9]] | accessdate=August 10, 2009 }}</ref> Davies received [[Sponsor (commercial)|sponsorship]] from [[Volkswagen Group of America]] and the [[Sundance Film Festival]] to use for gas money, car expenses, and the film.<ref name=wkrn>{{cite web | url=http://www.wkrn.com/Global/story.asp?s=8159947 | title=Vandalism sparks nationwide campaign | date=April 12, 2008 | publisher=[[WKRN-TV|wkrn.com]] | accessdate=August 10, 2009 }}</ref> In 2009, Davies' documentary film, ''Fagbug'', premiered at [[film festival]]s throughout the United States.<ref name=vanity/>
On August 17, 2009, Davies announced on her website that she is considering ending the FagBug's reign, stating that she was tired of all the publicity and hate.
