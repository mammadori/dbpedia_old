The '''Ehrhardt-Szawe''' was a [[Germany|German]] [[automobile]] manufactured between 1924 and 1925.  It was formed when [[Ehrhardt (automobile)|Ehrhardt]] took over operations for the [[Szawe]] company of [[Berlin]]-[[Reinickendorf]].  The resulting car, a 2570cc 10/50hp ohc six-cylinder, was designed without regard to cost; even its [[radiator]] was made of German [[silver]].
