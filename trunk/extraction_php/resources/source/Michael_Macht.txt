'''Michael Macht''' (28  August 1960 &ndash;) is the [[CEO]] of the German car manufacturer [[Porsche]]. He was previously head of production on the [[Vorstand|management board]] and was appointed chairman of the management board of Porsche AG and Porsche Automobil Holding on 23 July 2009. He is also on the board of Porsche SE.
==Early life and career==
Michael Macht was born in [[Stuttgart]], [[Germany]] on 28  August 1960. He studied [[Mechanical Engineering]] at the [[University of Stuttgart]] and then worked at the [[Fraunhofer Society|Fraunhofer Institute for Industrial Engineering]], before joining Porsche in 1990, at first a specialist for engine planning. He later became assistant to the chairman of the management board, and in 1994 he was made managing director of Porsche Consulting. In 1998 he was appointed to the board of Porsche AG, responsible for production and logistics.<ref name="Porsche-PR">{{cite press release  | title = Macht appointed Chairman of the Board of Management for Porsche AG| publisher = [[Porsche]]  | date = 2009-07-23  | url = http://www.porsche.com/uk/aboutporsche/pressreleases/pag/?pool=international-de&id=2009-07-23 | accessdate = 2009-07-25 }}</ref> Macht was also directly involved in the design of Porsche's most controversial models, the [[Porsche Cayenne|Cayenne]] and the [[Porsche Panamera|Panamera]]. In July 2009 he was appointed chairman of the management board of Porsche AG, as successor to [[Wendelin Wiedeking]].<ref name="Porsche-PR"/>
