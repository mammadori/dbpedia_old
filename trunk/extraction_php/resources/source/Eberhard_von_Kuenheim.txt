Dr.-Ing. E.h. '''Eberhard von Kuenheim''' (born 2 October 1928) is a [[Germany |German]] industrial manager.  He was, between 1970<ref name=Autocar1976>{{cite journal| authorlink = | title = E. von Kuenheim (BMW) "Growth through flexibility". An exclusive interview with Edouard Seidler| journal = [[Autocar]]| volume = 145 (nbr 4172)| pages = pages 114 - 115 |date = date 23 October 1976}}</ref> and 1993, Chairman of the executive board (effectively [[CEO]]) of the [[BMW Group]].

== Biography ==
Kuenheim was born in [[Judyty, Warmian-Masurian Voivodeship|Juditten]] near [[Bartenstein]], ([[East Prussia]]). His father died of a riding accident in 1935<ref name= Ziesemer>{{cite web |url=http://books.google.de/books?id=Mnqv5eOdrvYC&pg=PA207&lpg=PA207&dq=eberhard+von+kuenheim&source=bl&ots=DT44bhLKBY&sig=BZFqw-S3ceDMvEoBCCGcl1E9WLU&hl=de&ei=aUP3SZ_TCJfhsAbS-9n_AQ&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=10#PPA210,M1|title=Pioniere der deutschen Wirtschaft |accessdate=2009-04-28 |author=Bernd Ziesemer |location= |work= |publisher= |year=2006 |language=German}}</ref><ref>[http://www.wiwo.de/unternehmer-maerkte/koepfe-der-wirtschaft/eberhard-von-kuenheim-577/ wirtschaftswoche.de]</ref>, his mother died in a [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] [[NKVD]] camp after [[World War II]]<ref name=Ziesemer/><ref>[http://www.handelsblatt.com/unternehmen/industrie/eberhard-von-kuenheim-der-adler-fliegt-am-besten-allein;1005265;0 handelsblatt.com]</ref>

Kuenheim fled the advancing [[Red Army]] in early 1945 and was [[Evacuation of East Prussia|evacuated]] via [[Pillau]] to Western Germany in March 1945 throughout the [[Operation Hannibal]].<ref name=Ziesemer/>

He studied [[Mechanical engineering]] until 1954 at the [[Universit√§t Stuttgart|Technical University of Stuttgart]]<ref>
[https://www.handwerkermarkt.de/nachrichten/themen-fur-handwerker/personalie-wer-was-wo-wie/vbw-ehrenpraesident-dr.-ing.-eberhard-von-kuenheim-wird-80/?searchterm=None handwerkermarkt.de]</ref> and joined the [[Herbert Quandt|Quandt Group]], BMW's largest shareholder since 1959, in 1965.<ref name=Ziesemer/>

On 1 January 1970 Kuenheim became the CEO of the BMW Group, when BMW had 23,000 employees. A number that had risen to 71,000 when he stepped down in 1993.<ref>[http://www.autonews.com/files/euroauto/inductees/kuenheim2004.htm autonews.com]</ref> Kuenheim "transformed BMW from a small, unfocused manufacturer of cars and motorcycles into a world performance luxury icon"<ref>
[http://automotivehalloffame.org/honors/index.php?cmd=view&id=687&type=inductees automotivehalloffame.org]</ref>

After [[Bernd Pischetsrieder]] followed him as CEO, Kuenheim guided BMW's supervisory board until 1999. Today he's the head of BMW's ''Eberhard-von-Kuenheim-Foundation'', he's a Honorary Senator of the [[Technical University of Munich]], which named a building at the Mechanical engineering faculty after Kuenheim.
